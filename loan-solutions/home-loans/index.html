Understand the Main Home Loan Choices

Before choosing a loan, it helps to understand how rate type, repayment structure and product features can affect your options.

Choice
What it means
Common benefit
Watch point
Fixed rate
Repayment rate is locked for a selected period.
More repayment certainty while the fixed period applies.
Break costs, limited extra repayments and fewer flexible features may apply.
Variable rate
Rate can move up or down during the loan term.
Often more flexible with offset, redraw and repayment changes.
Repayments can rise if interest rates increase.
Split loan
Part of the loan is fixed and part is variable.
Balances repayment certainty with some flexibility.
Needs careful structure and review when fixed period ends.
Interest only
Repayments cover interest for a period, not principal.
Can support cash-flow planning for some investors.
Total interest cost may be higher and lender rules are stricter.

Feature Checklist

The right loan structure depends on how you plan to repay, save, access funds and manage future changes.

  • Offset account to reduce interest on eligible loans
  • Redraw access for eligible extra repayments
  • Ability to make extra repayments
  • Fixed, variable or split repayment structure
  • Owner-occupier or investment policy and pricing
  • Pre-approval suitability before making offers

Documents Usually Requested

  • Photo ID and residency details
  • Payslips or income evidence
  • Bank statements and savings history
  • Details of existing debts and expenses
  • Property contract or estimated purchase details
  • Rental income evidence for investment loans
