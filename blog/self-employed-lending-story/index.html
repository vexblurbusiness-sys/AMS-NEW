How Business Owners Can Prepare a Clear Lending Story

Self-employed lending is easier to discuss when the documents explain how the business earns, reports and supports income.

Explain the business structure

Start with the basics: sole trader, company, partnership or trust. Lenders may need to understand who owns the business, how income is distributed and which entity earns the revenue.

Show income consistency

Tax returns, financial statements and BAS can help explain the pattern of income. If revenue has changed significantly, prepare the reason and supporting evidence.

Understand add-backs

Some expenses may be reviewed differently depending on lender policy. Add-backs are not automatic, so it helps to identify which expenses may be relevant and what evidence supports them.

Prepare the document pack

  • Personal and business tax returns where available
  • Financial statements and profit-and-loss details
  • BAS or business activity evidence
  • Business bank statements if requested
  • Company, trust or partnership details where relevant

Ask which pathway fits

Some borrowers fit a full-doc pathway, while others may need an alternative-doc review where lender policy allows. A broker can help compare what each lender is likely to accept before an application is submitted.

The strongest application is not just a pile of documents. It is a clear explanation of how the business income supports the loan.
