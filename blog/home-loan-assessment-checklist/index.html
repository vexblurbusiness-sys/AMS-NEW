What to Prepare Before a Home Loan Assessment

A useful first conversation starts with clear goals, realistic numbers and the documents lenders commonly ask to see.

Start with the goal

Before looking at lenders, clarify what you want the loan to help you do. Buying a first home, upgrading, investing, refinancing and accessing equity can all lead to different questions.

Useful details include the property type, estimated price, location, timing, deposit or equity position and whether the loan will be owner occupied or investment.

Prepare income and employment details

Lenders usually need to understand how your income is earned and how consistent it is. For PAYG employees, that may mean payslips and employment details. For business owners, the review may include tax returns, financial statements, BAS and company structure.

  • Recent payslips or income records
  • Bonus, overtime, commission or allowance evidence if relevant
  • Employment start date and role details
  • Business or trust details for self-employed borrowers

Know your expenses and debts

Borrowing conversations are easier when you can clearly explain living costs, existing repayments and credit commitments. Credit cards, personal loans, car finance and buy-now-pay-later accounts may affect lender assessment even if balances are low.

Show the money position

Savings history, deposit source, gifts, sale proceeds and equity can all matter. If family support is involved, ask early what evidence the lender may need.

What to do next

Once you have the basics, a broker can help test lender policy, identify likely document gaps and explain whether pre-approval, refinance or another pathway makes sense.

General information only. A lender decision depends on full assessment, documents, valuation, credit criteria and loan terms.
