When a Refinance Review May Be Worth Considering

A refinance review can help you compare whether your existing loan still fits your rate, cash flow, feature and property goals.

Your rate no longer feels competitive

If your rate has drifted above comparable options, a review can help compare what is available and whether switching costs are justified.

You need different loan features

Offset, redraw, split loans, fixed options and repayment flexibility can matter as much as the headline rate. If your current loan no longer supports how you manage money, a review may be useful.

Switching costs need to be counted

A cheaper rate is not automatically a better outcome. A careful comparison includes discharge fees, government fees, valuation costs, package fees, possible break costs and any change to the loan term.

You want to access equity

Equity access may be considered for investment, renovation or other approved purposes. The review should include property value, loan balance, income, purpose of funds and repayment impact.

The loan term is changing

Extending the loan term may reduce monthly repayments but can increase long-term interest cost. Ask to compare the short-term benefit with the full-term cost before deciding.

A refinance review is most useful when it compares your current loan, realistic alternatives and the reason you want to change.
