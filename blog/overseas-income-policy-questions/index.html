Borrowing with Overseas Income: Policy Questions to Ask

Foreign income can be accepted by some lenders, but the details of currency, residency, documents and employment can make a major difference.

Which currency is paid?

Lenders may treat currencies differently. Some currencies are more commonly accepted, while others may need a specialist lender review or may be shaded when converted to Australian dollars.

Where does the borrower live?

Residency, visa status, citizenship and tax residency can affect available lender options. If one applicant lives in Australia and another earns offshore income, the application may need a more detailed structure review.

What documents are available?

Overseas income applications often rely on a clean document trail. The stronger the link between employment contract, payslips, bank credits and tax documents, the easier it is to explain the income story.

  • Employment contract or employer letter
  • Payslips or salary certificates
  • Bank statements showing income deposits
  • Tax records where available
  • Translations where documents are not in English

How strong is the deposit?

Deposit size, source of funds and property purpose can influence lender appetite. Some scenarios may require a larger deposit or additional evidence compared with local PAYG income.

Questions to ask early

Ask whether the lender accepts your currency, how income is converted, whether translations are required, how foreign tax is treated and whether the application is suitable for owner occupied or investment lending.

Do not assume one lender's policy reflects the whole market. Overseas income lending varies significantly by lender and borrower profile.
