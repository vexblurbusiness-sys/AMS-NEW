First Home Buyer Documents: What Lenders Usually Ask For

Prepare the core documents that help a broker understand your deposit, income, purchase timing and first buyer pathway.

Identity and buyer details

Most applications start with identity documents and basic buyer details. If more than one person is buying, each applicant usually needs to provide their own documents.

Income evidence

For salaried buyers, recent payslips and employment details are common starting points. If your income includes overtime, commission, bonus, allowances or a second job, ask what evidence is needed before relying on it.

Deposit and savings

Lenders may ask how the deposit was built. Savings history, gifted funds, sale proceeds or family support may each need different evidence.

  • Bank statements showing savings pattern
  • Gift letter or family support evidence if relevant
  • Deposit amount and funds available for costs
  • Estimated purchase price and suburb or property type

Funds to complete

Deposit is not the only cost. Buyers should also consider stamp duty, legal costs, lender fees, inspections, adjustments and moving costs. Grants and concessions may help eligible buyers, but eligibility should be checked for your state and situation.

Pre-approval questions

Pre-approval can help clarify a buying range, but it is not the same as final approval. Final approval may still depend on updated documents, the property, valuation and lender conditions.

Before bidding at auction or signing a contract, ask how your approval conditions, deposit, valuation and settlement timing work together.
