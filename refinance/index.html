When a Refinance Review Makes Sense

If any of these situations apply, it may be worth reviewing your current loan before making a decision.

Check my loan
1Your fixed rate is ending
Review new repayments, revert rate, break costs and switching options.
2Your equity position changed
Explore whether equity can support renovation, investment or restructuring goals.
3Your loan features no longer fit
Compare offset, redraw, repayment flexibility or split structure options.
4Your repayments feel stretched
Review rate, term, repayment type and debt consolidation considerations carefully.

Documents for a Refinance Review

Prepare these details so your refinance review can focus on costs, savings, structure and timing.

  • Current loan statement and repayment details
  • Current interest rate, product type and fixed expiry date
  • Income evidence and recent bank statements
  • Property estimate and council or title details where available
  • Details of other debts if consolidation is being considered
