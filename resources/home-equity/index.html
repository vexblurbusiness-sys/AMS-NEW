Equity review

What the Team Will Usually Check

A useful equity review starts with the current property value estimate, loan balance, income and purpose of funds.

1Property position
Current estimated value, lender, loan balance and repayment type.
2Purpose of funds
Purchase, investment, renovation, debt consolidation or other goal.
3Repayment impact
How the new loan size affects cash flow and long-term cost.
4Structure
Whether split loans, offset, interest-only or new lender options should be compared.

Questions to Ask Before Using Equity

Equity can be useful, but it should be reviewed with the full borrowing picture in mind.

What is the purpose?Lenders usually want to understand the reason for additional borrowing.
What happens to repayments?More borrowing may increase repayments even if the rate looks competitive.
How accurate is the value?A lender valuation can differ from an online estimate or agent appraisal.
Is the structure right?Splits, offsets and investment-purpose loans should be reviewed carefully.
