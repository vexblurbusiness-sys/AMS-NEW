Rate and Loan Review

Compare More Than the Advertised Rate

A useful loan review looks at repayment type, fees, features, switching costs, loan purpose and whether the structure still fits your plans.

Request rate reviewEstimate refinance savings

What a Rate Review Should Include

Rate is important, but the best comparison is usually a full loan review.

Interest Rate

Compare current rate, available alternatives and whether the rate suits your loan purpose and repayment type.

Fees and Costs

Review annual fees, package fees, discharge fees, valuation costs, government fees and possible break costs.

Loan Features

Offset, redraw, split loans, fixed or variable options and repayment flexibility can affect everyday value.

Timing

Fixed rate expiry, settlement timing, cashback conditions and property valuation can all affect the decision.

When a Lower Rate May Not Be the Whole Answer

A lower rate can still be less useful if the loan term restarts unnecessarily, important features are lost, or switching costs outweigh the benefit.

Review Your Current Loan

Prepare the details below before asking for a comparison.

  • Current lender, loan balance and remaining term
  • Interest rate, repayment type and repayment frequency
  • Offset balance, redraw use and fixed rate expiry date if relevant
  • Annual fees, package fees and any known discharge or break costs
  • Your reason for reviewing: lower repayment, features, equity, consolidation or structure
