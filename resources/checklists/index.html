Prepare the Documents Lenders Commonly Ask For

Use these checklists to reduce back-and-forth and make your first assessment more useful.

Request tailored checklistRead FAQs

Core Documents

Most home loan conversations start with identification, income, savings, debt and property details.

Identity

  • Photo ID
  • Visa or residency details if relevant
  • Current contact details

Income

  • Payslips or income records
  • Employment details
  • Bonus, overtime or allowance evidence

Money Position

  • Savings history
  • Bank statements
  • Existing loan or credit card details

Property Goal

  • Purchase price or loan balance
  • Property use
  • Contract or listing details when available

Keep Sensitive Documents Protected

Before sending identity documents, confirm the secure channel the team wants you to use.

  • Avoid attaching sensitive ID documents to casual email unless a secure upload method has been provided.
  • Keep file names clear so each document is easy to match to the lender request.
  • Check that statements show your name, account details and relevant dates.
  • Ask if translations, certified copies or accountant letters are required before paying for them.
